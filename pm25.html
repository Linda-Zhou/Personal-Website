PM2.5 and hospitalization rate due to pulmonary hypertension

This is a special study project I did with Prof. Roger Peng in my first year graduate school study. The question is to quantify the association between PM2.5 and hospital admission for respiratory disease in two states in the US: California and Arizona.

The first time we met he gave me two publications on that topic and let me find five questions that I don't understand. I am curious about how missing values are handled since there are lots of missing values in our dataset. So we focused on how to handle missing values before we dived into the actual question.

[here]

This was the second time we met, I made a graph of the missing pattern by latitude and longitude. Then I found that missing values in California do not have obvious spatial pattern, but in Arizona, missing values are clustered in its two major cities. Then I fit a poisson model on missingness vs cases to see if it's missing at random or not. The coefficient associated with pm25 is not significantly different from 0. So we conclude that thereâ€™s no relationship between missingness and cases/primary (missing at random).

Then, what we did is that we included a weight of 1/(1-Pr_missing) when fitting the logistic regression. That is, give the non-missing observations more weight and vice versa.

Lastly, we decide to limit our population to only major cities (population > 2500). We found no positive correlation in major cities of Arizona.
